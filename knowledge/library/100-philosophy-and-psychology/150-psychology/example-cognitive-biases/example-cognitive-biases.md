# A Guide to Cognitive Biases in Decision-Making

> **Source:** [Example Blog — Jane Example](https://example.com/cognitive-biases-guide) · published 2025-03-15 · ingested 2026-04-17

## TL;DR

- Cognitive biases are systematic errors in thinking that affect decisions and judgments
- Awareness alone doesn't fix them — you need structural countermeasures (checklists, pre-mortems, devil's advocates)
- The most dangerous biases in professional settings are confirmation bias, anchoring, and sunk cost fallacy
- Teams make better decisions than individuals, but only when dissent is explicitly encouraged

## Key Concepts

- **Confirmation bias** — seeking information that supports what you already believe
- **Anchoring** — over-relying on the first piece of information encountered
- **Pre-mortem** — imagining a project has failed and working backward to identify causes

## Notable Quotes

> "The first step to better decisions is accepting that your brain is optimized for speed, not accuracy."
